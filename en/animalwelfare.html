Approach to
Laboratory Animal Welfare

How we work regarding laboratory animal welfare

PorMedTec supports the R&D of pharmaceuticals, medical devices, and regenerative medicine products utilising pigs to facilitate human health. We are also conscious about animal welfare and the importance of achieving this form a scientific point of view. We therefore established and follow the laboratory animal welfare regulations below to demonstrate appropriate handling of laboratory animals.

  1. Act on the Welfare and Management of Animals (1973)
  2. Act on the Conservation and Sustainable Use of Biological Diversity through Regulations on the Use of Living Modified Organisms (2003)
  3. The Basic Policy on Measures that should be Implemented Regarding the Welfare and Management of Animals (2006)
  4. Standards relating to the Care and Keeping of Animals Reducing Pain of Laboratory Animals (2006)
  5. Standards relating to the Methods of Destruction of Animals (2007)
  6. Basic Guideline for the Conduct of Animal Experiments in Implementing Agencies Under the Jurisdiction of the Ministry of Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries (2006)
  7. Basic Guideline for the Conduct of Animal Experiments in Implementing Agencies (2006)
  8. Basic Guideline for the Conduct of Animal Experiments in Implementing Agencies Under the Jurisdiction of the Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare (2006)
  9. Guideline for Proper Conduct of Animal Experiments (2006)
  10. Guide for the Care and Use of Laboratory Animals; Eighth Edition (National Research Council, 2011)

Conducting animal experiments

We breed and keep pigs used in animal experiments for our R&D with the principle of 3R (Refinement, Replacement and Reduction) in mind.

  1. Refining experiments on animals by minimising animal discomfort. (Refinement)
  2. Where possible replacing animal experiments by methods not requiring animals. (Replacement)
  3. Reducing the number of animals involved per experiment. (Reduction)

Approach to genetically recombinant DNA experiment

We ensure safety regarding recombinant DNA experiment by following the Act on the Conservation and Sustainable Use of Biological Diversity through Regulations on the Use of Living Modified Organisms (2003).
